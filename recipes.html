Healthy Recipes

Total time: 20 mins.
Cook time: 10mins.
Prep time: 5 mins.
Servings: 3 - 4

Ingredients

  • ½ Almond flour
  • 2 tbsp Cup coconut flour
  • 1/2 tsp baking powder
  • Pinch of salt
  • ¼ cup almond milk (or any milk of choice)
  • 2 tbsp organic, cold pressed coconut oil
  • 3 eggs
  • 2 tbsp honey
  • ½ tsp pure vanilla extract
  • Grass-fed butter for cooking
  • ½ Blueberries fresh or thawed from frozen are best to use
  • Maple syrup (Optional)

Instructions:

  1. In a small bowl, combine almond flour, coconut flour, baking powder and salt
  2. In a large bowl, thoroughly whisk together the eggs. coconut oil and honey together
  3. Combine wet and dry ingredients, fold in the blue berries.
  4. Melt a dab of butter in frying pan over medium heat. Cook ¼ cup pancake portions for 2 minutes on the first side or until edges have set and bottom is golden. Flip and cook for 1 minute on the other side or until golden and cooked through.
  5. Once cooked serve immediately with some drizzle of maple syrup (optional) on top or cool the pancakes completely before refrigerating or freezing for later.











Prep time: 15 mins
Total time: 15 mins
Servings: 4

Ingredients

Salad:

  • 1 cup uncooked quinoa
  • 8 ounces fresh cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1 large cucumber, chopped
  • ¼ cup red onion, finely chopped
  • 5 ounces fresh spinach, roughly chopped
  • 2 large ripe avocados, pit removed and chopped
  • ¼ of 1 bunch cilantro, optional and to taste
  • 1/3 cup feta cheese, optional and to taste

Dressing:

  • 4 tbsp red wine vinegar
  • 2 tbsp Dijon mustard, do not use regular mustard
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • 1 Clove garlic, minced
  • ½ cup olive oil
  • 1 lemon (2 – 3 tbsp fresh lemon juice)
  • Salt and pepper

Instructions:

  1. Cook the quinoa according to package directions. Fluff and set aside to cool.
  2. Meanwhile, prep the dressing. Whisk the red wine vinegar, Dijon mustard, oregano, garlic, ½ tsp salt (or to taste), and ¼ tsp pepper (or to taste) together in a small bowl. Slowly add in the olive oil into the vinegar mixture while whisking briskly. Whisk in the lemon juice. Pour into a jar and store in the fridge while prepping the vegetables.
  3. Prep the veggies: Halve the cherry tomatoes, chop the cucumber (peel if desired), finely chop a quarter of a red onion. Roughly chop fresh spinach, remove the pits and chop the avocados. Finely chop the cilantro if desired.
  4. In a large bowl, add in all the prepped veggies and quinoa. Remove the dressing from the fridge and shake it well and then pour over the salad. Toss the salad and then top with feta cheese if desired.
  5. Enjoy immediately.

Ingredients:

  • 2/3 cup honey
  • 1/2 cup melted butter or coconut oil
  • 1 Tbsp. vanilla extract
  • 3 eggs
  • 1 cup almond flour
  • 1/2 cup cocoa (I used raw cacao)
  • 1/4 tsp. baking soda (this can be omitted)
  • 1/4 tsp. sea salt (omit if using salted butter)

Instructions

  1. Heat oven to 350 degrees F.
  2. Mix honey, butter, vanilla and eggs until smooth. (If omitting baking soda, beat eggs until foamy before adding in other wet ingredients.) Add almond flour, cocoa, baking soda and optional salt. Stir to blend.
  3. Pour into greased 8x8x2 inch pan. Bake for approximately 25 minutes, until center no longer jiggles and top feels cakey.
  4. Cool on a wire rack at least until sides pull away from the edge of the pan before cutting.

Delicious healthy smoothie to help you loose weight. Great for breakfast!

Ingredients:

  • 1.5 cups of fresh spinach
  • 1 large handful of fresh mint
  • 3/4 cups of coconut water, or regular water
  • Juice of 2 limes
  • 1 frozen or freshly chopped banana
  • ice cubes (optional)
  • 2 teaspoons of honey (optional)

Instructions

  1. Add all ingredients into a blender together and blend until the mixture is smooth.
  2. Garnish the mixture with mint and lime
